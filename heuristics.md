# HEURISTICS

Quick-fire rules for in-the-moment decision making.

## Reception Over Transmission
- If you're thinking about what YOU want to say → STOP → Think about what THEY need to hear
- If the message feels "complete" without considering the receiver → it's self-expression, not communication

## The Nod Test
- If your audience can't nod along to your first three sentences → you started too high
- Count the nods before you introduce anything new

## The Mirror Test
- If your communication makes the receiver feel SOLD TO → rewrite to make them feel SEEN
- The best persuasion feels like self-discovery, not salesmanship

## The Example Test
- If you can't point to ONE concrete instance of your claim being true → don't make the claim
- No examples = no credibility = floating abstraction

## The One-Argument Rule
- If you have more than 3 supporting arguments → you haven't found the real one
- One devastating argument > ten mediocre ones
- If you feel the need to list reasons → you're engineering an answer, not finding the truth

## The Language Test
- If you're using words the audience doesn't use → you're an outsider speaking tourist language
- Match their tribal signals or don't enter their world

## The Simplicity Heuristic
- If a smart 15-year-old can't understand it → simplify
- Complexity is not depth. Simplicity is not shallow.
- The easier you make it, the further it scales

## The Scale Test
- If your numbers are outside the audience's daily experience → translate to their scale
- 5 lakh > 100 crore for most audiences (even though 100 crore is "more impressive")

## The Immune System Check
- If resistance increases with evidence → the immune system is active
- Switch to validation-first immediately
- Never fight the immune system head-on

## The Consistency Audit
- If any detail contradicts another detail in your world → fix it or remove it
- One inconsistency = permission to doubt everything

## The Prototype Heuristic
- If you're first and have no examples → build a prototype
- A prototype is partial proof that something could exist
- Partial proof > no proof > theory

## The Universe Size Rule (AI Prompting)
- Three-line prompt = barren universe = generic output
- The richness of your output is capped by the richness of your prompt universe
- Specify laws, entities, examples, anti-patterns, micro-details

## The Anchor Rule
- Your first sentence determines the trajectory
- Title + opening sentence = the most powerful anchors you have
- A boring anchor produces boring reception

## The Story Rule
- When choosing between a story and a statistic → always choose the story
- Wrap the statistic inside the story if you must use it
- "92% satisfaction" = forgotten. "Raj closed two extra deals last Friday" = remembered.

## The Ground Truth Rule
- How far can you stretch from what they believe WITHOUT lying?
- That distance is your reach from ground truth
- Maximize reach. Never cross into deception.
