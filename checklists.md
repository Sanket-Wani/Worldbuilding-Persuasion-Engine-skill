# CHECKLISTS

## Pre-Communication Checklist

Before writing, pitching, prompting, or presenting ANYTHING:

- [ ] **Audience identified** — Can I name the specific person/group I'm addressing?
- [ ] **World mapped** — Can I describe their current worldview in 3 sentences?
- [ ] **Language catalogued** — Do I know their in-group terms, jargon, tribal signals?
- [ ] **Identity mapped** — What self-image do they protect? What do they want to be true about themselves?
- [ ] **Psychological needs identified** — What do they ACTUALLY optimize for (not what they SAY they optimize for)?
- [ ] **Atomic units listed** — What are 3-5 things they already agree with that I can open with?
- [ ] **Examples prepared** — Do I have concrete case studies/instances for every claim?
- [ ] **Leverage point found** — Have I identified the SINGLE root assumption that matters most?
- [ ] **Simplicity verified** — Could a smart 15-year-old follow this?
- [ ] **Scale matched** — Are my numbers/references in their frame of reference?

---

## Post-Draft Review Checklist

After writing but before sending:

- [ ] **Reception test** — Am I expressing myself or engineering their experience?
- [ ] **Nod sequence check** — Do the first 3 sentences produce agreement?
- [ ] **Mirror test** — Does the receiver feel SEEN or SOLD TO?
- [ ] **Example density** — Does every major claim have at least one concrete example?
- [ ] **Consistency audit** — Does every detail obey the world's established rules?
- [ ] **Immune system check** — Is anything likely to trigger cognitive resistance?
- [ ] **Language match** — Am I using THEIR words or MY words?
- [ ] **Argument count** — Am I using ONE devastating argument or a laundry list?
- [ ] **Story wrapping** — Are statistics wrapped in narrative? Or floating naked?
- [ ] **Ground truth distance** — How far am I stretching from what they believe? Is it truthful?

---

## AI Prompt Quality Checklist

Before submitting any AI prompt:

- [ ] **Universe defined** — Have I specified what world this AI should inhabit?
- [ ] **Laws set** — Are there explicit constraints, rules, and boundaries?
- [ ] **Entities populated** — Are specific entities, relationships, and context provided?
- [ ] **Examples given** — Have I provided few-shot examples of desired output?
- [ ] **Anti-patterns declared** — Have I stated what the AI must NOT do?
- [ ] **Micro-specifics added** — Have I gone beyond surface-level into rich detail?
- [ ] **Dimension coverage** — How many dimensions of the output have I specified vs. left to defaults?

---

## Pitch / Proposal Checklist

- [ ] **Investor/decision-maker world mapped** — What math do they run? What outcomes do they need?
- [ ] **Their language used** — Am I speaking GTM, ARR, CAC, or their equivalent?
- [ ] **Nod sequence built** — Do I open with problems they already agree exist?
- [ ] **Frame shift prepared** — Is my deviation framed as extension, not contradiction?
- [ ] **Rooting ready** — Do I have case studies, prototypes, or comparable successes?
- [ ] **Single leverage argument** — Is there ONE reason that maps to their deepest optimization target?
- [ ] **Identity confirmation close** — Does the close mirror their desired self-image?

---

## Worldbuilding Depth Checklist

For any narrative, brand, or constructed reality:

- [ ] **Source world identified** — What existing world am I borrowing from?
- [ ] **Unique spin added** — What's my original element (the "sandworm")?
- [ ] **Micro-details consistent** — Do small details reinforce internal logic?
- [ ] **Rules established** — Are there clear "laws of physics" for this world?
- [ ] **Examples grounded** — Are there existence proofs within the world?
- [ ] **Cross-references working** — Do elements reference each other consistently?
- [ ] **Foreshadowing present** — Are future elements being set up in advance?
