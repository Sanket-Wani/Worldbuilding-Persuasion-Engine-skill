# ANTI-PATTERNS

Patterns to actively avoid. Each is the negation of a core principle.

## AP01: The Self-Expression Trap
**Pattern:** Writing to express your thoughts/feelings/identity
**Why it fails:** Centers the wrong person. Communication is about engineering the receiver's experience.
**Fix:** Reframe: "What experience do I want to create in THEIR mind?"

## AP02: The Transmission Fixation
**Pattern:** Optimizing word choice, grammar, syntax while ignoring how the message is received
**Why it fails:** A perfectly composed message that isn't received is worthless.
**Fix:** Test every line against: "How will the receiver experience this?"

## AP03: The Generic Copypaste
**Pattern:** Sending unresearched, one-size-fits-all communications
**Why it fails:** Signals laziness and outsider status. Gets filtered instantly.
**Fix:** Customize with audience-specific language, company names, tribal signals.

## AP04: The Outsider Pitch
**Pattern:** Attempting to convince a community you haven't studied
**Why it fails:** Without mapping their world, your message will be rejected by their cognitive immune system.
**Fix:** Run full Audience Deconstruction (F03) before any communication attempt.

## AP05: The Brute Force Contradiction
**Pattern:** Bombarding the audience with facts that contradict their worldview
**Why it fails:** Maximally activates the cognitive immune system. They dig in harder.
**Fix:** Frame Shift (F02) — validate first, modify incrementally, root with examples.

## AP06: The Engineering Answer
**Pattern:** Listing many reasons/points hoping one will stick
**Why it fails:** Dilutes force, signals low confidence, misses the one argument that matters.
**Fix:** Leverage Point (F04) — find the single root assumption. Use only that.

## AP07: The Floating Claim
**Pattern:** Making assertions without concrete examples or evidence
**Why it fails:** Unrooted ideas feel theoretical and unreal. The audience has nothing to grasp.
**Fix:** For every claim, provide at least one concrete instance. No examples → no claim.

## AP08: The Inconsistent World
**Pattern:** Constructing narratives with internal logic contradictions
**Why it fails:** One inconsistency breaks immersion and gives permission to doubt everything.
**Fix:** Audit every detail against established world-rules. Go micro.

## AP09: The Knowledge Curse
**Pattern:** Assuming the audience knows what you know
**Why it fails:** You omit context they need. Your message becomes opaque.
**Fix:** Constantly ask: "What does my reader know? What am I assuming? What's the simplest way?"

## AP10: The Lazy Prompt
**Pattern:** Giving AI vague, short, generic prompts
**Why it fails:** Creates a barren universe. AI fills unspecified dimensions with statistical defaults.
**Fix:** Worldbuild your prompt. Laws, entities, examples, constraints, anti-patterns.

## AP11: The Scale Mismatch
**Pattern:** Using numbers, frames, or references outside the audience's daily experience
**Why it fails:** The information doesn't compute in their operating system.
**Fix:** Translate to their scale. Use their units, their frames, their reference points.
